Instructions

  • Prepare bananas: Peel and slice bananas into thick rounds, about 1/2 inch thick. Place on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  • Make chocolate mixture: In a small bowl, mix together almond butter, cocoa powder, and maple syrup until smooth and well combined.
  • Assemble bites: Spread a small amount of chocolate mixture on half of the banana slices, then top with remaining banana slices to create sandwiches.
  • Add toppings: Press mini chocolate chips gently into the chocolate filling around the edges of each bite.
  • Chill and serve: Place in refrigerator for 10 minutes to set, then serve immediately or store in an airtight container for up to 3 days.

Frequently Asked Questions

How should I store these banana bites?

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. For longer storage, freeze for up to 1 month in a freezer-safe container.

Can I make these nut-free?

Yes! Substitute the almond butter with sunflower seed butter or tahini for a completely nut-free version that's still delicious.

Are these banana bites kid-friendly?

Absolutely! Kids love these sweet treats, and they're a healthy alternative to processed snacks. The natural sweetness from bananas makes them appealing to little ones.

Can I use different sweeteners?

Yes, you can substitute maple syrup with honey, agave nectar, or date syrup. Start with the same amount and adjust to taste.

Can I freeze these banana bites?

Yes! These freeze beautifully for up to 1 month. Place them in a single layer on a baking sheet to freeze individually, then transfer to a freezer bag.

What's the best banana ripeness for this recipe?

Use ripe bananas that are yellow with a few brown spots. They should be sweet and soft but not mushy for the best flavor and texture.
