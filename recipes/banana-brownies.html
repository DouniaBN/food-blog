3 Ingredient Banana Brownies (Healthy) (soft, gooey and naturally sweetened)

these 3 ingredient banana brownies are one of those recipes that makes you stop and think: wait, is it really just those three ingredients? yes, it really is. they're soft, gooey, and genuinely fudgy in a way that feels far more indulgent than the ingredient list suggests. they're also naturally sweetened, entirely free from added sugar, and come together in about five minutes of prep.

the key to making these banana brownies as sweet and flavourful as possible without adding any sugar is choosing the right bananas. the riper the better, and by riper i mean those spotty, heavily speckled ones that you've been putting off using. as bananas ripen, their starches convert to natural sugars, making them significantly sweeter and softer. overripe bananas also blend much more smoothly, which is why these brownies end up with such a uniform, fudgy texture rather than lumpy pockets.

the almond butter is doing a lot of heavy lifting in this recipe. it acts as the binding agent that holds everything together, adds a creamy richness to the batter, and contributes healthy fats that make each brownie genuinely satisfying rather than leaving you reaching for more ten minutes later. those fats also help slow down the digestion of the natural fruit sugars in the bananas, giving you a more steady energy release rather than a spike and crash.

cacao powder is what gives these brownies their deep chocolate flavour. unlike cocoa powder which is roasted at higher temperatures, raw cacao retains more of its natural compounds including flavonoids and antioxidants associated with supporting heart health. it is also naturally rich in magnesium, which many people are deficient in, and has been linked to mood support. so yes, these are genuinely good for you, and they taste amazing.

blending the ingredients rather than mashing them by hand is what takes these from good to really good. a blender removes every trace of banana chunk and creates a silky smooth batter that bakes up into a fudgier, more cohesive brownie. you can absolutely mash by hand if you don't have a blender, but the texture will be slightly more rustic and a little less uniform.

once baked, patience is the most important part of this recipe. these brownies continue cooking and firming up as they cool, and they will fall apart completely if you try to slice them while still warm. let them cool fully at room temperature first, then chill them in the fridge for even better results. cold from the fridge is actually my favourite way to eat them: they firm up beautifully, the chocolate flavour feels richer and more developed, and each slice holds together cleanly. try my chocolate chip banana bread bars next if you're on a banana baking spree!

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I store banana brownies?

Store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. The texture actually improves after a few hours of chilling because the brownies firm up and the chocolate flavour deepens. Let them sit at room temperature for a few minutes before eating if you prefer a slightly softer texture, or enjoy them straight from the fridge for a firmer, fudgier bite.

Can I freeze these banana brownies?

Yes, these freeze really well. Slice them first, then store in a freezer-safe airtight container or zip-lock bag for up to 2 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight or at room temperature for about 30 minutes before eating. You can also enjoy them straight from the freezer for a firmer, almost ice-cream-bar-like texture, which is honestly delicious in summer.

Do these banana brownies taste like bananas?

Yes, the banana flavour comes through noticeably alongside the chocolate. If you love the chocolate and banana combination you will really enjoy these. If you want a more neutral chocolate flavour, using very overripe bananas actually helps because the sweetness becomes more caramel-like and less distinctly banana as the sugars develop. The cacao also balances the fruit flavour significantly.

Do I need a specific brownie pan to make these banana brownies?

No specific pan is required. Any small oven-safe baking dish works well. A smaller container will give you thicker brownies with a fudgier centre, while a larger dish makes them thinner and they will bake faster. If using a larger tin, start checking them from the 12 minute mark. The top should look set and no longer appear wet in the middle.

Can I make these banana brownies without almond butter?

Yes. Cashew butter works really well as a more neutral-flavoured alternative. Peanut butter also works but adds a distinct peanut note alongside the chocolate and banana, which some people love. For a nut-free version, sunflower seed butter is a solid substitute, though it can give the brownies a slightly more savoury edge. Tahini could work in a pinch but may slightly compete with the cacao flavour.

Why do banana brownies need to cool completely before slicing?

These brownies continue to cook and set as they cool. When they come out of the oven they are very soft and will fall apart completely if you try to slice them while still warm. Once fully cooled at room temperature they hold together much better, and after a stint in the fridge they become properly sliceable with clean edges and a satisfying fudgy texture.

Can I add chocolate chips to these banana brownies?

Absolutely, and highly recommended. Add chocolate chips or chunks on top before baking so they melt into soft, gooey pools as the brownies cook. You can also stir a small handful into the batter before transferring to the pan if you want chocolate throughout every bite. Dark chocolate or refined sugar free chips both work beautifully.

Pro Tips

  • Let the brownies cool completely before slicing, ideally with a stint in the fridge. They are very soft straight from the oven and firm up into their fudgy final texture as they cool.
  • Use the ripest bananas you can find. Heavily speckled or almost completely blackened bananas produce the sweetest, smoothest batter and the best flavour.
  • Use a blender rather than mashing by hand for the smoothest, most fudgy result. Even a few banana lumps will create an uneven texture in the finished brownie.
  • Finish with a pinch of flaky sea salt just before serving. The contrast between the salt and the naturally sweet chocolate brownie is one of the best flavour combinations.

Instructions

  • Step 1: Preheat the oven to 170°C / 160°C fan (340°F).
  • Step 2: Add the bananas, almond butter, and cacao powder to a blender and blend until smooth. Do not add the chocolate chips yet if using.
  • Step 3: Transfer the batter to a brownie baking tray or small oven-safe baking dish. Add chocolate chips or chunks on top.
  • Step 4: Bake for 15 to 20 minutes until the top is set.
  • Step 5: Allow to cool completely before removing or slicing. They will continue cooking as they cool and will break apart if removed while still hot.
  • Step 6: Sprinkle with sea salt and enjoy!
